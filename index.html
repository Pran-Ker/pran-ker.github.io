.03 featured · paper

            
REAL — Benchmarking autonomous agents on deterministic simulations of real websites.

          


          

            

              

                

                

                

                

                
arXiv : 2504.11543

                
REAL

                
Benchmarking Autonomous Agents on
Deterministic Simulations of Real Websites

                

                  Prannay Hebbar · Divyansh Garg · Shaun VanWeelden · Diego Caples · Nikil Ravi · Pranav Putta · James Liu · Atharva Gundawar · Christian Schroeder de Witt · Sumeet Motwani · Naman Garg · +7
                

                

                  
11sites

                  
112tasks

                  
~41%frontier cap

                  
18authors

                

                

                  NeurIPS 2025 · San Diego
                  
                  submitted 2025.04.15
                

              

            


            

              

                A high-fidelity benchmark of deterministic replicas of 11 real-world websites — with 112 verifiable tasks. The point: most agent evals are too rubric-driven and non-reproducible. Determinism gives you a ladder; rubrics give you noise.
              

              

                
contrib
First author. Designed the task suite, built the deterministic replicas, ran the frontier-model evals.

                
finding
Frontier models top out near 41% — agentic competence on real-feeling web tasks lags far behind chat competence.

                
why
If we want agents that ship value, we need evals that don't lie. Determinism > rubric grading.
