.03 featured · paper

            
REAL — deterministic simulations of real websites for agent benchmarking.

          


          

            

              

                

                

                

                

                
arXiv : 2504.11543

                
REAL

                
Benchmarking Autonomous Agents on
Deterministic Simulations of Real Websites

                

                  Prannay Hebbar · Divyansh Garg · Shaun VanWeelden · Diego Caples · Nikil Ravi · Pranav Putta · James Liu · Atharva Gundawar · Christian Schroeder de Witt · Sumeet Motwani · Naman Garg · +7
                

                

                  
11sites

                  
112tasks

                  
~41%frontier cap

                  
18authors

                

                

                  NeurIPS 2025 · San Diego
                  
                  submitted 2025.04.15
                

              

            


            

              

                Most agent evals are rubric-driven and non-reproducible. REAL replaces them with deterministic high-fidelity replicas — same starting state every run, machine-checkable outcomes. The point isn't a leaderboard; it's a ladder.
              

              

                
contrib
First author. Designed the task suite, built the deterministic replicas, ran the frontier-model evals.

                
finding
Frontier models top out near 41% — agentic competence on real-feeling web tasks lags far behind chat competence.

                
why
If we want agents that ship value, we need evals that don't lie. Determinism > rubric grading.

.06 principles

            
Things I believe in — opinions, loosely held.

            
Source: /principles/beliefs · personal arc: /principles/about

          


          
    
            
  1. 
              i.
              
    
                
    Shipping fast beats the best strategy.
    
                
    Speed is a superpower. Bias toward shipping. Listen, build, ship, tell the customer, then repeat.
    
              
    
            
    2. 
            
  2. 
              ii.
              
    
                
    You have no career ceiling.
    
                
    Grit > talent. There's no substitute for the hours. Get 1% better every day.
    
              
    
            
    3. 
            
  3. 
              iii.
              
    
                
    Be ruthlessly truth-seeking.
    
                
    Strong opinions, loosely held. The truth can be painful — you can just change your mind when wrong.
    
              
    
            
    4. 
            
  4. 
              iv.
              
    
                
    Communication is the job.
    
                
    Clear writing is clear thinking. Leaders step up to provide clarity when it's absent. Mismatched expectations lead to sadness.
    
              
    
            
    5. 
            
  5. 
              v.
              
    
                
    Leadership means owning outcomes beyond the org chart.
    
                
    Influence > titles. Do the work yourself and delegate. Write your own playbook.
    
              
    
            
    6. 
            
  6. 
              vi.
              
    
                
    Work can also be your hobby.
    
                
    Passion + boundaries beats the myth of "work-life balance." Best work follows curiosity.
    
              
    
            
    7. 
            
  7. 
              vii.
              
    
                
    Demos > memos.
    
                
    You could have built a prototype during the meeting. Only ship things you're excited about yourself.
    
              
    
            
    8. 
            
  8. 
              viii.
              
    
                
    Hiring is what separates good leaders from great.
    
                
    Two answers: hell yes, or no. Hire people you can learn from, and would someday be happy working for.
    
              
    
            
    9. 
            
  9. 
              ix.
              
    
                
    Assume good intent.
    
                
    Lead with empathy. They might just be having a bad day. Criticism is good feedback if you listen unemotionally.
