UniPrefill: block-wise dynamic sparsification gives up to 2.1× TTFT on long-context LLMs, any architecture

Most long-context speedups assume vanilla full attention. UniPrefill is an architecture-agnostic prefill accelerator that sparsifies at the token block level and integrates as a vLLM continuous-batching operator — it works on hybrids (linear+full, sliding+full) where prior sparse attention fails. Reports up to 2.1× TTFT, with speedup that scales with concurrent requests.

Training recipe

  • Base: any long-context decoder LLM; tested across attention variants including hybrids.
  • Data: standard long-context inference workloads (not detailed in abstract).
  • Compute: not reported.
  • Loss / algo: training-free — block-wise dynamic sparsification at the token level during prefill.
  • Schedule / hparams: integrated as a vLLM continuous-batching operator with PD co-processing and tensor parallel.
  • Eval: TTFT speedup vs dense prefill on long-context loads with varying concurrency.

Key learnings

  • Up to 2.1× TTFT at long context on standard architectures.
  • Speedup scales with concurrency — more in-flight requests amplify the win.
  • Hybrid-friendly. Works for linear/full and sliding/full hybrids where attention-only sparsification breaks.
  • vLLM-native. Continuous batching, PD co-processing, tensor parallel supported out of the box.
  • No retraining. Block-wise token selection is decided at inference time per request.

Use when

  • Serving long-context chat or RAG at high concurrency on shared GPUs.
  • Running hybrid-attention models (Mamba+attn, sliding+full) where standard sparse attention degrades.
  • You're already on vLLM and want a prefill knob without rebuilding the serving stack.
