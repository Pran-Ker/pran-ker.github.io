Curated synthetic data needn't collapse — if you curate with a Nash bargain over multiple rewards

Prior work says recursive retraining on reward-curated synthetic data inevitably collapses unless you mix in real data. This paper proves otherwise: curate with multiple reward functions and the limiting distribution is a weighted Nash bargaining solution that preserves output diversity while still optimizing performance.

Training recipe

  • Base: generic generative model in a recursive-retraining loop.
  • Data: synthetic samples curated by reward(s); no required real-data mixing.
  • Compute: theoretical analysis with empirical illustration (no large-scale training claimed).
  • Loss / algo: curate by multiple rewards (preferences); analyze the limiting distribution under repeated retraining.
  • Eval: convergence to a stable distribution; diversity across high-reward regions.

Key learnings

  • Curation pluralism saves you. With ≥2 rewards, the limit distribution allocates mass across all of their high-reward regions instead of collapsing.
  • It's literally Nash bargaining. The fixed point is a weighted Nash bargaining solution — a principled aggregation rule, not an ad-hoc fix.
  • You don't have to inject real data. Past wisdom said real data was the only escape from collapse; this gives a recipe that uses only synthetic data.

Use when

  • You're building a self-improvement pipeline (LLM curation, RLAIF, data synthesis) and want a theory-backed knob to avoid mode collapse.
